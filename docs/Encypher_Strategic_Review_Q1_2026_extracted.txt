3. Key Strategic & Messaging Insights
The following insights emerged from conversations and have operational implications. Many are not yet captured in internal documentation.
3.1 Validated Strategic Shifts
Platform Distribution Model (ICP 1C) Validated as Primary GTM: Freestar, Mediavine, Aditude, Snack-Media, and WordPress VIP conversations confirmed that signing infrastructure distributed through publisher-facing platforms is dramatically more capital-efficient than one-by-one publisher coalition building. Each platform partner provides access to hundreds of publishers through a single integration. This is now the primary GTM motion.
Doc Impact:  ICP 1C exists in docs but is underweighted relative to its actual strategic importance. Should be elevated to co-equal with ICP 1A.
Full Standards Stack Mapping (Scott Cunningham): Scott Cunningham mapped four distinct standards layers: IAB Tech Lab CoMP (operational specifications), NMA (contract terms), AAM (quality audits), and C2PA (watermarking/tracking). C2PA is the only layer that 'travels with content' beyond publisher boundaries. This is a fundamental positioning insight.
Doc Impact:  Not in any document. Needs dedicated section in GTM Strategy and Marketing Guidelines.
SPUR Coalition (UK) Connection: BBC, Financial Times, Guardian, Sky News, Telegraph forming UK coalition implementing the full standards stack. Charlie Hatford (BBC) is Encypher's connection point as the most active publisher contributor to C2PA text standard. Scott Cunningham predicts global replication in 2-3 years.
Doc Impact:  Not in any document. Should be added to ICP doc and GTM Strategy as international expansion vector.
Encypher Attest Product Line: Cryptographic authentication product for enterprise AI-generated content built across three development phases. Targets legal, financial services, and healthcare markets. Word/Outlook plugins, compliance dashboards, policy engines built. Partially demoed to Waldo Capital.
Doc Impact:  Not in ANY project document. This is an entire product line with no internal documentation.
Syracuse Symposium Postponed: Only three RSVPs received. Key participants had conflicts with AI Impact Summit in New Delhi. Reframed as intentional expansion. 1:1 briefings offered as substitutes.
Doc Impact:  All 8 documents still reference February 25 as upcoming. Every reference needs updating.
Copyright Registration Determines Enforcement Ceiling: $150K statutory damages per work applies ONLY to registered works. '3x damages' language was corrected to '$150K per registered work' across all materials in February.
Doc Impact:  Correction was applied to docs in February but some residual '3x damages' references may remain. ROI Framework in Enterprise Sales doc still says '3x damages potential.'
Detection Capability Is Web-Surface Only: Encypher cannot track content through AI training pipelines. Detection is web-surface scraping/republication. All messaging about 'finding content in AI outputs' was corrected.
Doc Impact:  Correction was applied in February. Needs verification that no overclaims remain.
Publisher-First Website Messaging: Publishers don't see themselves as part of an 'AI Content Economy.' They see themselves fighting unauthorized AI use. Website audit recommended shifting from AI-first to publisher-first framing. Content theft/competitor scraping added as explicit value proposition.
Doc Impact:  Website messaging audit completed but documented insights need to be formalized in Marketing Guidelines.

5. Team & Operational Insights
5.1 Matt Kaminsky Performance
Matt has been a strong hire. In his first week, he generated six qualified conversations with sales houses, publishers, and ad tech companies. He successfully synthesized the value proposition in his own words during alignment calls and asked the right qualifying questions. His former employer relationships (Mediavine, broader ad-tech network) are producing warm introductions.
The current operating model is intentional: Erik joins Matt's meetings during a training period (approximately six hours total), after which Matt handles Tier 2-3 conversations independently. The Snack-Media meeting was graded C+ with specific coaching notes around Erik over-presenting and missing qualification questions. Matt's strengths are social proof and relationship leverage; areas for development are deeper technical explanation and discovery discipline.
5.2 Nate Alvord Status
Nate (Contract CRO, 5% equity, 1-year cliff) underperformed relative to expectations in the first six weeks. After a performance conversation in February, he committed to outreach using Erik's C2PA contact list and his ProPublica contact, with emails reviewed by Erik first. No shares vest until the one-year cliff, giving Erik significant leverage. The situation is being managed carefully.
5.3 Erik's Operating Patterns (Self-Assessment Themes)
Multiple meeting debriefs identified a consistent pattern: Erik tends toward over-presenting rather than conducting discovery. Key qualification questions (publisher count, decision-making process, budget authority) are frequently missed when Erik is in presentation mode. The Snack-Media and Apartment Therapy debriefs both flagged this. Matt's training period is partially designed to create forcing function for Erik to practice shorter explanations.
Erik also acknowledged bandwidth as the primary constraint. He is simultaneously handling product development, standards work, fundraising, BD, content marketing, investor relations, and all Tier 1 relationships. The strategic question is not 'what should we do' but 'what can Erik physically execute given 24-hour days.'
6. Competitive & Market Intelligence
6.1 IP3 Digital Assessment
Amyli McDaniel's IP3 Digital (formerly MINTangible) was assessed as architecturally inferior to Encypher for content distributed through uncontrolled channels. Encypher's approach of embedding licensing terms directly into content via C2PA manifests eliminates the marketplace friction inherent in IP3's middleware model, survives edge distribution, and benefits from standards-body backing versus IP3's proprietary framework. Recommendation: do not pursue formal partnership. Monitor for potential conflation in market.
6.2 WordProof Distinction
WordProof timestamps publication dates on blockchain. Encypher embeds provenance in text that survives copy-paste. Different problems, different mechanisms. Risk is conflation by publisher GCs who hear 'content authentication' and think they've seen this before. FAQ content recommended to address this proactively.
6.3 Litigation Intelligence
Active litigant research identified priority outreach targets: Media News Group (Alden Global Capital subsidiary, active OpenAI/Microsoft litigation), The Philadelphia Inquirer (part of 14-publisher Cohere collective action). Secondary targets with high exposure but no current litigation: Fandom, Valnet (now in active conversation), Andrews McMeel Universal (syndication model is strong fit for watermarking use case).
6.4 Aditude Channel Intelligence
Jared Siegal provided rich market intelligence during meeting. Key takeaways: LMC board meeting includes Nexstar, Tegna, Media News Group, Gray Media (Tobias Bennett is operational executor, not Scott Cunningham). Prebid.org has an AI working group with bi-weekly publisher calls. CPMStar sellers.json is a prospecting resource. Rob Beeler's publisher Slack is a community channel. Approximately 40% of Aditude's base is gaming publishers with no editorial content, making them poor Encypher targets.
