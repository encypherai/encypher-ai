### Official Color Palette

**IMPORTANT: These colors are fixed and should NOT be changed. Our logo uses Deep Navy as the primary color.**

**Primary Colors:**
- **Deep Navy (#1B2F50):** Our signature color from the logo. Use for backgrounds, headers, and text on light backgrounds. Conveys security, professionalism, and trust. Should dominate large areas like banners and footers.
- **Azure Blue (#2A87C4):** Our primary brand highlight color from the logo (the "AI" text). Use for buttons, links, CTAs, and any element that needs to stand out. Signals innovation and clarity.
- **Light Sky Blue (#B7D5ED):** Secondary backgrounds, infographics, hover states. Provides soft contrast against navy. Used in the logo behind the padlock icon. Creates openness and breathing room.

**Accent & Supporting Colors:**
- **Cyber Teal (#00CED1):** *RECOMMENDED REPLACEMENT for Soft Rose.* Use sparingly for security-focused highlights, alerts, or important notifications. Aligns with cybersecurity industry standards while providing strong contrast. Use in charts, icons, or to draw attention to critical information.
- **Neutral Gray (#A7AFBC):** Body text, secondary text, strokes, and lines. Use instead of pure black for a softer, modern look. Ensures readability while maintaining approachable tone.
- **Pure White (#FFFFFF):** Backgrounds and whitespace. Essential for clarity and cleanliness. Generous white spacing communicates transparency and keeps content uncluttered.

**Typography:**
- **Primary Font:** Roboto (all weights)
- **Headings:** Roboto Bold or Medium
- **Body Text:** Roboto Regular
- **Code/Technical:** Roboto Mono

**Color Usage Guidelines:**
- Deep Navy: 40-50% of design (backgrounds, headers, primary text)
- Azure Blue: 20-30% (CTAs, links, highlights)
- Light Sky Blue: 15-20% (secondary backgrounds, subtle accents)
- Cyber Teal: 5-10% (critical alerts, security-focused elements)
- Neutral Gray: Body text and supporting elements
- White: Generous whitespace throughout

**Never Use:**
- Pure black (#000000) - too harsh
- Bright red - too aggressive for our collaborative tone
- Neon colors - unprofessional
- Colors outside this palette without executive approval
