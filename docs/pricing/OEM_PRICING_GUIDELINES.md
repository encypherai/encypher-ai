# OEM / Non-Publisher Pricing Guidelines

**Status:** Internal Only
**Last Updated:** December 3, 2025
**Owner:** Sales / Product

---

## Overview

This document provides internal pricing guardrails for **OEM and non-publisher customers** who want to integrate Encypher APIs into their products or use content provenance for internal compliance purposes.

**Key distinction from publisher tiers:**
- OEM / non-publisher customers do **not** participate in the Licensing Coalition.
- They pay a **platform fee + usage-based component** rather than receiving coalition revenue share.
- All OEM deals are **sales-driven** (not self-serve).

---

## Target Segments

### 1. SaaS / Platform Integrators
- **Profile:** Enterprise SaaS products embedding Encypher APIs (CMSes, legal tech, knowledge platforms, AI tools).
- **Use case:** Provide content provenance as a feature to their end users.
- **Value:** Differentiation, compliance, trust signals for their customers.

### 2. Internal Enterprise Use
- **Profile:** Large enterprises using provenance for internal content governance.
- **Use case:** Audit trails, regulatory compliance (EU AI Act, China watermarking), internal content tracking.
- **Value:** Compliance infrastructure, risk mitigation, operational visibility.

## Pricing Posture

### Base Platform Fee
| Volume | Annual Base Fee | Notes |
|--------|-----------------|-------|
| Pilot / POC | $10K–$25K | 60–90 day evaluation |
| Standard | $25K–$50K | Typical mid-market |
| High Volume | $50K–$100K+ | Large-scale deployments |

### Usage-Based Component
| Metric | Rate | Notes |
|--------|------|-------|
| C2PA Signatures | $0.001–$0.01 per signature | Volume discounts available |
| Sentence Tracking | $0.0001–$0.001 per sentence | For Merkle/attribution use |
| API Calls | Included in base | Up to agreed quota |

### No Coalition Revenue Share
- OEM customers do **not** receive coalition payouts.
- Their content is **not** added to the licensing corpus.
- They pay for infrastructure, not for coalition participation.

---

## Negotiation Levers

When negotiating OEM deals, consider:

1. **Volume commitments:** Higher minimums = lower per-unit rates.
2. **Contract length:** Multi-year = better pricing.
3. **Feature scope:** Merkle/attribution vs. basic C2PA only.
4. **Support tier:** Standard vs. dedicated TAM.
5. **Deployment model:** SaaS vs. on-premise (on-prem adds 50–100% premium).
